Morris — the mill family

The morris, or mill, family is a line of placement-and-movement games: each player places stones, then slides them along the board’s lines; completing a mill — three in a row — lets you capture an opponent’s stone, and you lose when reduced below three men or left without a legal move. The family scales from a trivial 9-point board up to the 24-point boards of nine men’s, Lasker, and Morabaraba, whose solution databases run to ~10¹¹ positions.

A clean pattern runs through it: the diagonal-free morrises — six men’s, nine men’s, Lasker — are all draws under perfect play; adding diagonal mills (Morabaraba, the twelve men’s morris) tips the game to a first-player win. The tiny three men’s board, which has diagonals, is also a first-player win.

This project’s contribution is six men’s morris: it was absent from the morris-solving literature — which had solved its larger siblings but skipped it — so this is its first published strong solution (a draw, over all 42,372,745 reachable positions).

Play the solver

Section titled “Play the solver”

The explorer plays four, five, or six men’s morris on the 16-point board (switch with the toggle) from a precomputed perfect-play tablebase — six men’s is this project’s result. Every move is labelled win, loss, or draw under perfect play.
